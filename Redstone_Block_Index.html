Data compiled from the Minecraft Wiki (piston pushing, falling blocks, water, mob spawning, hitboxes, conductivity) and established Java Edition redstone mechanics, current as of mid-2026 (~1.21.x). Grouped by behavior family — see a row's expanded notes for material-specific caveats. Piston collapses the piston interaction into one of three states: Movable (relocates intact), Immovable (piston refuses to extend — nothing moves or breaks, e.g. Obsidian, chests), or Breaks (the piston extends but destroys the block, e.g. Torch, Cobweb). Height and Width/Length reflect the block's actual collision hitbox (not just its visual model) wherever the two differ — e.g. Honey Block is 15px, not a full 16px cube. Conductive follows the Wiki's "Conductivity" property: whether a block can be powered and pass a redstone signal through itself. Opaque full blocks (stone, wood, most ores) generally are conductive; transparent blocks (glass, ice) and partial/non-full blocks (slabs, stairs, fences) generally aren't — plus a handful of named exceptions that go against the grain, like Glowstone and Honey Block (Java) being non-conductive despite being full cubes, or Soul Sand and Mud being conductive despite not being full-height. Flammable is a simplified indicator covering the common, well-documented cases (wood, wool, leaves, vines, etc.) — treat it as a starting point rather than an exhaustive list. Bedrock Edition differs on several of these mechanics and isn't covered here. Always verify truly load-bearing builds in-game.
